Dental Hygiene and Therapy

Dental therapy involves providing more advanced treatments for dental problems, such as fillings, extractions, and other restorative procedures. Dental therapists work under the supervision of dentists and can provide many of the same services that dentists do. Dental hygiene and therapy play a vital role in maintaining good oral health and preventing dental problems before they become more serious.

Tooth Structure

The ability to recognize the normal radiographic appearance of the teeth and their component structures is foundational to basic interpretation of intraoral and panoramic radiographic images. It is of equal importance to recognize when the radiographic appearance of the teeth deviates from normal.

Basic Radiation

The purpose of this course is to present basic concepts of radiation, energy conversion and the generation of x-rays, essential components of an x-ray unit, characteristics of the x-ray beam, and image formation.

Demineralization/ Remineralization

This course is an introduction to the concepts of demineralization and remineralization and how they can and should be incorporated into clinical practice. Upon completion of this course, participants will understand the continued need for fluoride to help protect against the ongoing challenge to tooth structure, secondary lesions and root caries.

Dentistry

Taking care of your dental health can help you maintain a healthy and beautiful smile for years to come. Remember to make dental hygiene a part of your daily routine and to seek professional care when necessary. With proper care, you can keep your teeth and gums healthy and strong.
